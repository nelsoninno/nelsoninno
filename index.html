About — 01

A founder shaped by two continents, building for many.

Nelson Inno is a Salvadoran entrepreneur, the founder and CEO of WeSpark, and the author of Unstable Innovation. He builds AI-powered education systems for large enterprises, governments, and universities, and co-designed Bitcoin Diploma 2.0 with El Salvador's National Bitcoin Office, a curriculum the country's Ministry of Education plans to bring to up to 500,000 students over the coming years.

His work has been featured in Forbes Centroamérica, ARD, ZDF, Cointelegraph, and El Salvador's press. His TEDx talk, Are Schools Prepared for the Future?, explores the same question that has guided his career: how learning has to change if the people who learn through it are going to thrive in the world they will actually live in.

Now — 02

What he's building now.

Corporate training has a structural problem. The best workplace learning has always come from direct, personal relationships between skilled facilitators and the people in the room. A great trainer knows each participant: their role, their gaps, their blind spots. That knowledge is what converts information into behavior that actually sticks. With time, that model became too expensive to scale. Organizations moved to webinars, e-learning modules, and slide decks built for hundreds at once. A platform cannot know a thousand employees the way a trainer knows twelve. The numbers tell the story: completion rates for asynchronous e-learning average 3 to 6 percent, 87 percent of employees forget training within 30 days, and the $400 billion corporate learning industry measures the wrong thing entirely: clicks, not comprehension. AI makes it possible to fix that.

The work draws on everything that came before it: the workshop methodologies and corporate training frameworks refined across seven years of WeSpark engagements with Fortune 500 clients, the visual-first pedagogy proven by Bitcoin Diploma 2.0 at national scale, and the conviction formed early in Nelson's career and reinforced in his TEDx talk that organizations which redesign how they train will outpace those that only redesign what they train. The opportunity is to bring the attentive, dialogical quality of expert human coaching to every team in every enterprise that has ever had to prove its people actually learned something.

Nelson's career has moved consistently in one direction: from technical foundations toward systems that change how people learn and work. He trained as an industrial engineer and earned an MBA in Sales and Service Engineering at Furtwangen University in Germany. His corporate years took him through Microsoft, Siemens, Avianca, Aeroman, and Marquardt, culminating in the role that pushed him toward entrepreneurship: Senior Innovation Manager at Lufthansa, where he designed and built four new innovation departments and strategies for multiple Lufthansa Group companies within three years. He founded WeSpark in March 2019 and has led it ever since, first from Frankfurt, then from San Salvador.
Philosophy — 08

How he operates.

Nelson's professional life is organized around three directional preferences and three operating principles.

The preferences sound simple and are not: slow rather than fast, but with the intention, direction, and focus that move you further than anyone else. Quality rather than quantity, because the right quality brings abundance in the things that matter. Calmness rather than noise; and yet, when the moment calls for it, knowing how to make people genuinely excited.
